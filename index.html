For Ashu 💫

Some moments don’t really leave… they just settle somewhere within you, quietly.
22nd June 2024 — it was just ice cream.

Nothing extraordinary on the surface.
But somewhere between the pauses, the glances, the easy silence…

something shifted.

Like happiness didn’t arrive loudly—
it just… sat beside me, and felt like you.
28th June felt different.

We spoke a little more honestly,
like we had both decided—without saying it—
to stop holding back.

And somewhere between words and quiet understanding…

we kissed.

Not rushed. Not uncertain.

Just enough to say
what neither of us had the courage to say before—

that we liked each other.
7th July, at IIT…

That day didn’t feel like a continuation—
it felt like something deepening.

Like whatever this was between us
had found a language of its own.

It wasn’t just a moment.

It was presence.
It was intensity.

It was something that stayed,
long after we walked away from it.
August in Chennai…

Those five days didn’t feel real.

Not because they were extraordinary—
but because of how effortless everything was.

No trying. No pretending.

Just us, existing in the same space,
like it had always been that way.

Like time, for once,
wasn’t in a hurry to move forward.
And then December…

That night wasn’t just closeness.

It was warmth that lingered,
comfort that didn’t need to be explained.

For a while,
the world felt quiet enough
for me to feel everything fully.

And I did.
We had something rare.

Not perfect.
Not permanent.

But real in a way that didn’t need proof.

And maybe that’s why it was hard to hold onto—
because some things aren’t meant to stay,
just to be felt.
I understand why things ended.

And somewhere along the way,
I stopped asking for a different ending.

Some stories don’t need closure.

They just become a part of you,
in ways you don’t always notice—
but never really lose.
Because some people…

they don’t leave completely.

They just become
a quiet presence in your memory,
showing up in the smallest moments.
